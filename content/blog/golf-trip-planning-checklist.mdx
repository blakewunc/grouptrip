Planning a golf trip for 8 people is a project. Between locking in dates, booking tee times, finding lodging, managing RSVPs, coordinating travel, tracking expenses, and running the betting formats — there's more moving pieces than most people expect.

Miss a step and something goes wrong: the tee time you wanted is sold out, someone's room is double-booked, or half the group doesn't know what they owe until three weeks after the trip.

This checklist runs from initial idea to wheels up. Use it, adapt it, and check things off as you go.

---

## 6 Months Out: Set the Foundation

### Lock in the basics

- [ ] Pick the destination — don't let perfect be the enemy of good. Scottsdale, Pinehurst, Myrtle Beach, Bandon Dunes — pick one and commit
- [ ] Narrow dates to 2–3 options that work for most people
- [ ] Send a poll to the group to confirm dates (not a group text — a Doodle or a dedicated poll)
- [ ] Set a response deadline: "Let me know by [date] or you're out"

### Establish the core group

- [ ] Confirm who's in. Organize around who can actually commit, not who says "probably"
- [ ] Identify the organizer (it's you) and one co-organizer to help with logistics
- [ ] Create a shared group chat dedicated to the trip

### Start course research

- [ ] Research the must-play courses at your destination
- [ ] Check tee time availability at top choices (some book 60+ days out, others 30)
- [ ] Note peak vs. off-peak pricing — a $100/person difference across 3 rounds adds up

---

## 4–5 Months Out: Book the Big Things

### Tee Times — Book First

- [ ] Lock in tee times for your anchor course (the one that books fastest)
- [ ] For groups of 8, secure two consecutive tee times (8 minutes apart is standard)
- [ ] Confirm the cancellation policy — will you get a refund if someone drops out?
- [ ] Book remaining rounds, prioritizing by demand/availability

### Lodging — Book Second

- [ ] Decide: resort rooms, rental house, or mix?
- [ ] For 8+ players, compare cost of hotel rooms vs. large rental house (rental often wins)
- [ ] Book lodging with flexible cancellation where possible
- [ ] Confirm check-in/check-out times work around your tee times

### Collect Deposits

- [ ] Send a deposit request to the group — $200–300/person is standard
- [ ] Give a clear payment deadline: "Venmo @organizer by [date] to hold your spot"
- [ ] Anyone who misses the deadline gets replaced. Hold the line.
- [ ] Use deposits to cover tee time deposits and lodging down payments

---

## 3 Months Out: Fill in the Details

### Travel Coordination

- [ ] Share dates with the group so everyone can book flights
- [ ] Identify who's flying vs. driving
- [ ] Coordinate arrival times — can everyone arrive within 2–3 hours of each other?
- [ ] Book airport transportation: rental van, shuttle, or rideshare budget

### Food and Dinner Planning

- [ ] Pick restaurants for group dinners (book ahead — popular spots fill up)
- [ ] Decide which meals are group meals vs. individual (usually: group dinners, individual lunches)
- [ ] Confirm any dietary restrictions

### Betting Formats

- [ ] Decide on the primary betting format: Nassau, skins, Stableford, or mix
- [ ] Set stakes that work for everyone — ask before assuming
- [ ] Decide gross or net (handicap-adjusted) — collect current handicap indexes from all players
- [ ] If running matches (2v2, 4v4), decide on pairings format

---

## 6–8 Weeks Out: Logistics and Communication

### Confirm Everything

- [ ] Reconfirm tee times with the pro shop (especially for large groups)
- [ ] Reconfirm lodging reservation
- [ ] Share the full itinerary with the group: who's arriving when, where you're staying, tee time schedule

### Collect Handicap Information

- [ ] Gather official handicap indexes from all players (GHIN or equivalent)
- [ ] Calculate course handicaps for each course on your schedule
- [ ] Determine stroke allocations for net betting

### Set Up Expense Tracking

- [ ] Choose how you'll track expenses (The Starter, spreadsheet, etc.)
- [ ] Log all pre-trip costs: tee time deposits, lodging down payments
- [ ] Clarify what's shared and what's individual before the trip starts

---

## 2–4 Weeks Out: Final Checks

### Logistics

- [ ] Confirm flight details for everyone and create a group arrival/departure schedule
- [ ] Arrange ground transportation: rental vehicle pickup, shuttle bookings
- [ ] If renting a van, assign a designated driver for each day

### Shopping and Prep

- [ ] Restock golf balls (budget 4–6 per round per player if it's a new course)
- [ ] Confirm you have enough sunscreen, tees, gloves
- [ ] Check that everyone has soft spikes if required by destination courses
- [ ] Purchase travel insurance if the trip is expensive enough to warrant it

### Communication

- [ ] Send a final itinerary to the group: lodging address, tee times, dinner reservations, driving directions
- [ ] Share emergency contact info with anyone who's flying solo
- [ ] Confirm everyone knows what they owe for pre-trip expenses

---

## 1 Week Out: Final Details

- [ ] Download offline maps or GPS routes to all courses
- [ ] Check weather forecast — adjust tee times or packing if needed
- [ ] Confirm all restaurant reservations
- [ ] Send final packing reminder to the group (it helps — someone always forgets clubs)

---

## During the Trip

### Day 1

- [ ] Get everyone checked into lodging
- [ ] Collect any remaining deposits or pre-trip payments
- [ ] Confirm tomorrow's tee time and arrival plan
- [ ] Run the pre-trip rules meeting: betting format, stakes, match pairings

### Every Day

- [ ] Log expenses in your tracking app as they happen
- [ ] Track match results after each round
- [ ] Send nightly score/standings update to the group (they love this)

### Last Day

- [ ] Generate expense summary and share with the group
- [ ] Collect all outstanding payments before checkout
- [ ] Do a quick room sweep for forgotten gear
- [ ] Send Venmo requests for anyone who still owes

---

## Post-Trip

- [ ] Confirm all payments received within 48 hours of return
- [ ] Send a trip recap (The Starter generates this automatically)
- [ ] Poll the group on 2026 destination before the glow fades

## The 10 Things That Derail Golf Trips

Most problems come from the same handful of mistakes:

1. **Booking too late** — prime tee times sell out 60+ days ahead at top destinations
2. **Not collecting deposits** — people drop out when there's no financial commitment
3. **Assuming everyone knows what they owe** — send an explicit breakdown
4. **No cancellation plan** — who pays when someone backs out last minute?
5. **Mismatched expectations on budget** — send a cost estimate upfront
6. **No designated organizer** — decisions need an owner
7. **Group text logistics** — create a dedicated channel or it gets lost
8. **Skipping the rules meeting** — betting disputes ruin rounds
9. **Not packing for the weather** — Scottsdale in November = 50°F mornings
10. **Waiting until after the trip to settle expenses** — do it on the last day

Plan well, track expenses, run the Nassau, and the trip handles itself.
