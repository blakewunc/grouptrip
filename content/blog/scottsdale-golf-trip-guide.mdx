Scottsdale is the most popular golf trip destination in the United States, and it's not particularly close. More than 200 courses within an hour. Weather that's reliably excellent from October through May. Enough restaurants, bars, and activities to keep non-golfers happy. And a resort infrastructure built specifically to handle groups of 8–20 with minimum friction.

If you're planning a golf trip and haven't done Scottsdale, start here.

---

## When to Go

**October–November:** The sweet spot. Temperatures drop back to the low 80s after summer heat, courses are in peak shape from fall overseeding, and crowds are manageable. Book 3–4 months ahead.

**February–March:** Peak season. The PGA TOUR's signature events bring serious crowds and premium pricing. Green fees at top courses spike to $300–500+. If you can swing it, the vibe is unmatched — but book 6+ months ahead and expect to pay.

**April–May:** Getting hot. Daytime highs push 95°F+ in May. Courses offer early morning tee times and afternoon rates, so it's workable with smart scheduling. Green fees drop 20–30%.

**June–September:** Too hot for most groups. Temperatures exceed 110°F. For heat-resistant golfers only. Some courses go into summer dormancy. Green fees are at their lowest.

## The Courses

### Tier 1: If Budget Is No Object

**Whisper Rock Golf Club** (Private)
You're not getting on here without a member. But if someone in your group knows a member, call in the favor. One of the finest desert course experiences in the country.

**TPC Scottsdale — Stadium Course**
Host of the WM Phoenix Open. Walking the fairways where Jon Rahm and Scottie Scheffler compete is genuinely cool. The par-3 16th hole (surrounded by stadium seating) is one of the most iconic holes in American golf. Book 60+ days out.

**Estancia Club**
Tom Fazio masterpiece. Dramatic elevation changes, Sonoran Desert views, and immaculate conditioning. Private, but accessible through caddie programs and reciprocal arrangements. Worth pursuing.

### Tier 2: Premium Accessible Courses

**Troon North Golf Club — Monument Course**
Desert mountain golf at its most dramatic. Massive boulders integrated into fairways, sweeping views, and some of the best conditioning in the Valley. One of the signature Scottsdale experiences for visiting golfers. Green fees: $200–350 depending on season.

**We-Ko-Pa Golf Club — Saguaro and Cholla**
Two excellent courses on Yavapai Nation land northeast of Scottsdale. Both are underrated, well-priced relative to quality, and less crowded than resort courses. Cholla was voted one of Golf Digest's top courses in Arizona. Green fees: $125–200.

**Grayhawk Golf Club — Talon Course**
Tom Fazio again. More traditional desert layout than Monument Course — wider fairways, more forgiving. Great for mid-handicappers who don't want to spend all day in the desert scrub. Also a good choice for groups with mixed skill levels. Green fees: $150–250.

### Tier 3: Great Value

**Papago Golf Course**
City of Phoenix municipal course with a Sonoran Desert setting that punches way above its price point. Redesigned by Schmidt-Curley. Green fees: $40–80. Perfect for a warmup round or an off-day round without breaking the budget.

**Dove Valley Ranch**
Well-conditioned, scenic desert course with reasonable pricing. Good for first-timers to desert golf. Green fees: $80–130.

## Where to Stay

### Westin Kierland Resort
The default choice for group golf trips. Walkable to upscale shopping and dining, ~15 minutes to most major courses, and well-equipped to handle large groups. Golf packages available. Rates: $250–500/night depending on season.

### Four Seasons Scottsdale at Troon North
For groups that want resort-within-a-resort. Direct access to Troon North, stunning desert settings, excellent pool. Rates: $400–800/night. Best if Troon North is your anchor course.

### Fairmont Scottsdale Princess
On-site TPC Scottsdale (Stadium and Champions courses). Massive resort campus — feels like its own small city. Great for groups that want to minimize driving. Home of the WM Phoenix Open week. Rates: $300–600/night.

### Rental Houses — The Smart Play

For groups of 8–12, a large rental house in North Scottsdale is often the better move. VRBO has dozens of properties in the $1,000–2,500/night range with pools, outdoor kitchens, and enough bedrooms for everyone. The math almost always works out cheaper than individual resort rooms, and you get a private space for the inevitable post-round gathering.

## Logistics

### Getting There

Phoenix Sky Harbor (PHX) is the main hub — connected to every major city. Most courses are 25–40 minutes north of the airport via the 101 freeway.

For groups of 8+, renting a 12-passenger van or minibus for the trip is worth the $200/day. It keeps everyone together, eliminates the drunk driving variable, and lets the group experience debrief properly on the way home from each round.

**Uber/Lyft surge note:** Early morning tee times (6:30–7:30 AM) often coincide with high surge pricing. Factor this in if you're not renting a vehicle.

### Tee Time Strategy

Most Scottsdale courses let you book 30–60 days in advance as a non-member. For peak February/March dates, call courses directly rather than booking online — some keep a block of prime tee times for phone reservations.

For groups of 8, you'll need two consecutive tee times. 8 minutes apart is standard. Some courses offer a shotgun start for groups of 16+, which keeps everyone together and speeds up play.

### Cart Rules

Most desert courses require carts and path-only rules in mornings when the desert scrub is wet with dew. This matters for groups accustomed to riding in the fairway. Plan for slightly longer rounds if cart paths are firm.

## Sample 5-Day Itinerary

**Day 1 (Thursday):** Arrive Phoenix, settle into rental house, dinner at FnB or Mastro's City Hall

**Day 2 (Friday):** Round 1 — TPC Scottsdale Stadium Course, 8:00 AM. Afternoon: pool. Dinner at Mission Scottsdale

**Day 3 (Saturday):** Round 2 — Troon North Monument, 7:30 AM. Group dinner at home — hire a private chef via Goldbelly or similar

**Day 4 (Sunday):** Round 3 — We-Ko-Pa Cholla, 9:00 AM (more relaxed day). Old Town Scottsdale evening — walk Craftsman Court, dinner at Virtù

**Day 5 (Monday):** Optional: Papago warmup/scramble, 7:00 AM. Checkout and flights

## What to Pack

- **Sunscreen:** The desert sun is relentless. Apply before you leave the house.
- **Layers:** November mornings in Scottsdale are 50°F. By noon it's 75°F. Dress in layers.
- **Extra balls:** Desert rough is a one-way trip. Budget for losing 4–6 balls per round until you're calibrated.
- **Golf shoes:** Most premium courses require soft spikes. Metal spikes will get you a polite but firm redirect to the pro shop.

---

## Final Word

Scottsdale is the rare golf destination that works for every type of group. Budget is flexible (Papago to TPC), lodging is flexible (rental house to Four Seasons), and the courses are genuinely great across all tiers.

Plan it 3–4 months ahead for fall travel, 6+ months for February/March. Book lodging before tee times. And build in one free afternoon — you'll want it.
