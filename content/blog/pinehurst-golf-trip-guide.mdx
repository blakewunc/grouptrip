Pinehurst, North Carolina is the closest thing American golf has to St Andrews. Eight championship courses on one property, a village that feels frozen in time, and the kind of golf culture that makes you want to leave your phone in the room and just play. If your group hasn't been, this is the trip.

Here's everything you need to plan it right.

---

## Why Pinehurst?

Most golf destinations ask you to pick between quality and quantity. Pinehurst doesn't. You can play a different championship course every day for a week and not repeat yourself. The village has everything you need within walking distance. The weather in May and September is nearly perfect.

For a group of 8, it's also surprisingly manageable. Pinehurst Resort handles large groups well — they're used to it.

---

## The Courses

### Pinehurst No. 2 — The Must-Play

Par 70, 7,588 yards from the tips. This is the one. Donald Ross's masterpiece. It hosted the U.S. Open in 2014 and will host again in 2024. The bermuda greens are unlike anything you've played — crowned, fast, and unforgiving. Miss a green and you're chipping up a slope. Make a birdie and you'll remember it.

**Book this first.** Tee times fill up months in advance, especially in spring and fall.

### Pinehurst No. 4 — The Runner-Up

Tom Fazio's redesign of No. 4 opened in 2018 and it's spectacular. Long, challenging, and a bit more forgiving than No. 2. This is the course your higher handicaps will actually enjoy. Par 72, slightly more generous landing areas, same stunning Sandhills scenery.

### Pinehurst No. 8 — For the Scratch Players

Tom Fazio designed No. 8 specifically as a "resort course" — meaning it's hard. Really hard. It rewards length and precision. If your group has a couple of 5-handicaps who want to be humbled, send them here.

### Mid Pines and Pine Needles — Off-Resort Options

If you're staying a fourth day, Mid Pines and Pine Needles (sister Ross courses about three miles from the resort) offer a different feel at a lower price point. Both are fantastic, both have excellent pro shops, and both have hosted major championships.

---

## Lodging

### The Carolina Hotel — Stay Here

Built in 1901, the Carolina is the anchor of the resort. Classic rooms, a great bar (1895 Grille), and a short walk to the practice facilities. For a golf trip with 8 players, booking a block of rooms in the Carolina keeps everyone together. Rates run $350–600/night depending on season.

### The Manor Inn — More Affordable

If budget is a consideration, the Manor Inn is 100 yards from the Carolina and offers similar access to the resort at roughly 30% less per night. Comfortable, clean, and perfectly functional for a golf trip.

### Rental Houses — For Larger Groups

Groups of 10+ often do better renting a house in the village. Several large homes are available through VRBO and allow you to cook breakfast, drink on the porch, and keep the logistics simple. Add about 20 minutes to your morning routine getting to the first tee.

---

## When to Go

**April–May:** Best weather. Courses in top shape. Book 4–6 months ahead.

**September–October:** Equally good. Summer heat has broken. Peak season crowds.

**June:** Hot and humid. Not ideal, but cheaper and more available.

**December–February:** Off-season pricing, but courses may close for overseeding.

## Sample 4-Day Itinerary

**Day 1 (Thursday)**
- Arrive Pinehurst
- Check in, unpack, walk the village
- Warm up on the putting green
- Dinner: 1895 Grille at the Carolina (book ahead)

**Day 2 (Friday)**
- Round 1: Pinehurst No. 2 — 8:12 AM tee time
- Lunch at The Deuce (the turn bar at No. 2)
- Afternoon: practice facility, putting contest, cold beers
- Evening: cards on the porch

**Day 3 (Saturday)**
- Round 2: Pinehurst No. 4 — 9:00 AM tee time
- Golf shop browsing
- Group dinner: Duddingsworth & Whitby or The Tap Room

**Day 4 (Sunday)**
- Optional 9 holes on No. 1 (walking, relaxed)
- Checkout by noon
- Drive home

## Logistics and Planning

### Tee Times

Book tee times directly through the Pinehurst Resort website. Members get priority access; non-members can book 60 days out. For groups of 8, you'll need two consecutive tee times (usually 8 minutes apart). Call the pro shop directly to confirm group pairings.

**Tip:** For No. 2, consider an early morning slot (7:00–8:30 AM). The light is better, it's cooler, and the course is empty.

### Getting There

Pinehurst is roughly equidistant from Charlotte, Raleigh, and Fayetteville airports. Charlotte (CLT) is the most connected hub if flying in. It's a 1.5-hour drive.

Several groups rent a 15-passenger van for the trip, which simplifies airport logistics and keeps everyone together.

### Splitting Costs

A 4-day, 8-person Pinehurst trip typically runs:
- Green fees (2 rounds/person): $350–600/person
- Lodging (3 nights, Carolina): $400–600/person
- Meals and drinks: $150–250/person
- Miscellaneous: $50–100/person

**Total: $950–$1,550 per person depending on course selection and lodging choice.**

### Betting Formats

Pinehurst is made for Nassau. Standard format: $5 per side, $5 for 18. Handicap index-adjusted.

For groups with mixed handicaps, consider a Stableford format for the No. 2 round — it keeps the high handicaps engaged even when the course beats them up.

## What to Pack

- Soft spikes only (resort policy)
- A sweater — mornings in April can be 50°F
- Sunscreen (the Sandhills sun is no joke)
- Cash for the caddie (if you're taking one — you should)
- Extra balls for the waste bunkers on No. 2

---

## Final Word

Pinehurst is not a destination you visit once. Groups that go once tend to make it an annual trip. The courses are good enough that you'll want to come back and play differently each time. Plan it right the first time, and it'll become a tradition.
